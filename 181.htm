@fuse2dx Wiggle cables near each end to check for a break. It could just be the cable that's broken. Failing that, turn off the speakers and open them up, check inside for obvious broken wires or any components surrounded by brown gunk (they've exploded). If a wire's come off a circuit board and you can see where it belongs, you can solder it back. If a component's blown, see if you feel confident identifying it, buying a replacement and replacing it. Otherwise, it's time for a multimeter/oscilloscope and lots of patience, checking all the terminals you can get access to.
