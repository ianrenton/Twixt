@CampaignReboot On-screen keyboards. Desktop wordprocessors assume that the user is hitting the right keys, so just do text-replacement on common misspellings such as receive/recieve. With an OSK even expert users still hit the wrong keys, so they employ heuristic algorithms to do autocorrect, e.g. user typed "pwt", that could be pat/pet/pit/pot/put but "e" is close to "w" so it's probably "pet".
