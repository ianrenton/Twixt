y = tan((x-0.5)*pi)+0.5 would give you the shape and the x-scale, but not the y-scale (it would tend to -Inf at x=0 and +Inf at x=1). If you want your graph to be perfectly vertical at the edges, I'm not sure that's really possible, but if you could settle for some lesser angle, it would be. The easiest is probably y = (tan((x-0.5)*pi/2)/2)+0.5, but that's a really shallow angle that doesn't look very curvy at all.
