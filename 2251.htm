There's not a lot of conclusive research on the origins of cold shock - I think it's sort of assumed that it's an involuntary muscle spasm caused by the temperature change. If you mean evolutionary origins, I'm not sure it has any - it's certainly not going to be selected for in cold aquatic environments, as cold shock regularly causes drowning and cardiac arrest. There's an evolutionary trait called the mammalian dive reflex ([link]; which is almost the opposite of the cold shock reaction that is a survival trait. There is some research ([link]; showing that repeated cold shocks increase the brain's ability to keep control over the body during them, reducing the risk of a gasp. Cold shock definitely happens when body parts other than the face enter cold water. Cover your mouth and nose when you jump :)
